# Can the grief be spoken *with* mother — not just *around* her?

> [!important] State at last update (2026-05-21)
> `arc_status: partial`. The 2026-05-09 phone call (70 min,
> client-initiated, regret-loop disclosed; mother's reciprocal
> disclosure of father's pre-death intent-to-repair) is the
> first sustained breach of the
> [[avoidant-mother-contact]] pattern. **One call is not pattern
> resolution.** Whether the new cadence stabilises across the
> next 3-6 months is the sub-question. The mother's grief —
> distinct from Mark's grief about the father — has not yet been
> sustained-ly *held* across multiple calls; that is the next
> layer.

## The question, refined

Three nested sub-questions, in order of difficulty:

1. **Can Mark initiate contact with mother?** *(answered:* yes,
   on 2026-05-09; behavioural data point exists.*)*
2. **Can Mark disclose his own grief content to mother?**
   *(partial:* yes for the regret-loop content on 2026-05-09;
   not yet tested for the cottage-cheese / "I can't picture
   the version of me who would have said the words" content.*)*
3. **Can Mark sustain a *containment* role for his mother's
   grief across multiple calls?** *(open.* The 2026-05-09 call
   was largely Mark *speaking-into-distress*; his mother's
   grief was disclosed in the context of disclosing
   information *about the father*, not as the call's primary
   content. The capacity to be present to his mother's grief
   over an hour, repeatedly, is the next attachment-layer
   update.*)*

## Why this question matters

The avoidance loop is the per-week marker of the
[[complicated-grief]] PGD trajectory in this case
([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han#^[22:30]]]). Han named the
mid-July SSRI decision as keying on this loop's loosening.
*Partial* loosening from one call is meaningful but
insufficient — the 3-6 month follow-on cadence is what
distinguishes a transient post-session compliance breach from
a real pattern shift.

The question is also the **most important attachment-layer
update** available in the arc. The dismissing-attachment
substrate ([[2026-04-02-session-reyes-analysis#working-formulation]]
*(attachment-theory lens)*) limits Mark's bandwidth to
*co-regulate* affect with a distressed attachment figure. The
mother is the closest-relationship distressed attachment
figure in his current field. Capacity to hold her grief
without retreating is the most concrete marker of
attachment-substrate modification in adulthood.

## What would close the question

`arc_status` would flip to `answered` upon **all** of the
following holding over a 3-6 month window:

- **Sustained client-initiation cadence**: Mark calls mother
  weekly (or at a mutually-agreed cadence) without
  voicemail-default returning as the dominant pattern.
- **Sustained mother's-grief holding**: Mark holds extended
  segments of mother's affective expression (crying, anger,
  longing) without retreating to outside-the-conversation
  recovery (the porch-with-a-beer pattern after the 20-min
  call) as the dominant post-call regulator.
- **Disclosure-fluency**: Mark discloses Mark's grief content
  (not only information *about* the father) without requiring
  external prompts.
- **No re-consolidation under stress**: a brief stress event
  (work deadline, Theo illness, anniversary date) does not
  collapse the pattern back to voicemail-default for >2 weeks.

If, by mid-July ~12-week post-loss, sustained-cadence is
holding and mother's-grief-holding has been demonstrated at
least once, the question moves to `answered` and the
[[medication-arc]] SSRI-review decision favours continued
hold. If the pattern re-consolidates back to baseline, the
question reverts to `open` and the SSRI question becomes more
active.

## What would change the question

If a **new** clinical event re-shapes the maternal relationship
materially (mother's own grief consolidation; a sibling /
extended-family conflict; mother's geographic move closer or
further from Mark), the question's scope expands and the
evidence rows above become a baseline rather than the full
picture. Re-ingestion of the relevant session(s) would update
this page in place.
