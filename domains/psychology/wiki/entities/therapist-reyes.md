## Role and frame

- **Modality positioning**: [[integrative-grief-therapy]] — does
  *not* identify as IFS-only, EFT-only, or attachment-only. Uses
  the modality that the in-room material calls for.
- **Pacing stance**: protector-led — does not push past the
  Manager-part's tolerance window. Session 1 deliberately
  contains no affect-contact attempt; session 4's
  meaning-reconstruction work only arrives after partial
  unburdening.
- **Cross-clinical posture**: actively coordinates with
  [[psychiatrist-han]] via brief written notes; does not duplicate
  diagnostic work; respects medication decision-making as Han's
  domain.

## Clinical signature observable across sessions

- **Body before words.** In every session, when the verbal channel
  reaches a constraint, Reyes pivots to a somatic question
  ("what's happening in your body when you say that?"). This is
  the EFT operating principle in practice.
- **Names the part, doesn't fight it.** When a protector behaviour
  is visible (the avoided phone call, the laughing at cottage
  cheese), Reyes names the part's *job* rather than the
  behaviour's *problem-ness*. IFS stance in practice.
- **Honest about uncertainty.** When the client asks
  *"how long is this going to last"*, Reyes does not produce a
  reassuring number; she names the actual uncertainty plus the
  honest worry-condition (six to nine months out, same
  intensity). [[2026-04-02-session-reyes]] [34:18]
- **Doesn't prescribe between-session homework.** Suggestions are
  framed as *experiments* the client may or may not run, with
  permission-to-not built in
  ([[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [30:08]).
