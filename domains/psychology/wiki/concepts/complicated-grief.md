# Complicated grief / Prolonged Grief Disorder (PGD)

> [!important] What this concept names
> A grief presentation that does **not** integrate on the typical
> 3-6 month timeline and instead consolidates into a sustained,
> impairing state organised around the loss. **DSM-5-TR
> (March 2022)** introduced *Prolonged Grief Disorder* (PGD) as a
> formal diagnosis with a **≥12-month** duration requirement in
> adults. Earlier literature uses the same phenomenology under labels
> like *complicated grief* (Horowitz / Shear) or *persistent
> complex bereavement disorder* (DSM-5 §III). All three labels are
> pointing at the same animal; the diagnostic gate is the
> 12-month duration.

This page tracks how the construct **appears in this worked-example
arc** and how its risk markers were used clinically *before* the
12-month gate could possibly be cleared — i.e. as **early-flag risk
factors** rather than diagnosis.

## Core features (per DSM-5-TR PGD criteria, summarised)

- **A. Identifiable bereavement** (death of someone close, ≥12
  months ago in adults, ≥6 months in children).
- **B. Grief response** marked by one or both of:
  - Intense yearning / longing for the deceased
  - Preoccupation with thoughts or memories of the deceased
- **C. Three or more (of eight) symptoms** in the past month, to a
  clinically significant degree:
  - identity disruption ("part of me died with them")
  - marked sense of disbelief about the death
  - avoidance of reminders that the person is gone
  - intense emotional pain related to the death
  - difficulty reintegrating into life
  - emotional numbness
  - feeling life is meaningless
  - intense loneliness
- **D. Significant impairment** (social, occupational, other).
- **E. Bereavement reaction clearly exceeds expected norms** for the
  individual's culture / context.
- **F. Not better explained** by MDD, PTSD, etc. (note: comorbid
  presentations are common; this is an *exclusion of "merely"*, not
  an exclusion of co-occurrence).

## Risk markers for PGD trajectory (used clinically before the 12-month gate)

The PGD diagnosis cannot be applied before 12 months. But four
markers reliably **predict** a PGD trajectory and warrant earlier
intervention:

1. **Sudden / unexpected loss** (e.g. cardiac death, accident,
   suicide) vs. anticipated loss with time to prepare.
2. **Unresolved interpersonal threads** between mourner and deceased
   — last interaction was a fight, longstanding tension never
   addressed, important things unsaid.
3. **Avoidance-cluster behaviours** appearing early — avoiding
   places / people / conversations connected to the deceased; in
   PCL-5 terms, items in the avoidance cluster scoring high
   sub-threshold.
4. **Pre-existing emotional-containment style** that limits the
   mourner's bandwidth to metabolise affect (e.g. men socialised
   in lineage cultures of unspoken affect; alexithymia traits;
   strong Manager-part dominance in IFS terms).

When all four markers are present at 2-3 months post-loss, the
trajectory has a **noticeably elevated probability of consolidating
into PGD** without intervention. This is the clinical logic Dr. Reyes
and Dr. Han apply across this arc.

## Differential

- **vs. Major Depressive Episode (MDD).** Both can co-occur. PHQ-9
  alone cannot distinguish; PGD's centre of gravity is the
  relationship to the deceased (yearning, identity disruption),
  while MDD's centre is pervasive low mood / anhedonia
  independent of the loss. Mark's "half-volume" presentation
  ([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han#^[08:22]]]) skews toward
  bereavement-coloured anhedonia rather than primary MDD; this is
  why Han defers SSRI initiation.
- **vs. PTSD.** Sub-threshold avoidance-cluster signal does not
  meet criterion A trauma (criterion-A requires a specific
  threat-to-life event; a parental MI is bereavement, not
  criterion-A trauma per current DSM-5-TR). The clinical use of
  the PCL-5 here is as a **dimension** of avoidance, not as a
  diagnostic instrument.
- **vs. normal grief.** Normal grief at 11 weeks can include all
  the same surface features. The distinction is **trajectory** —
  normal grief integrates by months 3-6; PGD trajectory shows the
  same features still active at month 6 and beyond. The clinical
  call at 11 weeks is necessarily probabilistic.

## Treatment implications visible in this arc

- **Process work over pharmacology in months 1-3.** Han defers
  SSRI initiation ([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han#^[22:30]]]) on the
  rationale that *grief is informational* and SSRI-driven
  affective flattening in the first six weeks of medication may
  blunt the integrative work happening in therapy.
- **Sleep treated as a separable problem.** Han offers trazodone
  for maintenance insomnia separately from any antidepressant
  consideration, because chronic sleep deprivation at 5 hours /
  night masks how much of the symptom load is grief proper vs.
  sleep-deprivation-pretending-to-be-grief.
- **Avoidance cluster as the per-week marker.** The avoidance loop
  (refusing maternal calls; not telling Sarah how he is) is the
  most actionable per-week target. Loosening of the avoidance
  cluster is the marker Han said he would check at 12-week
  follow-up ([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han#^[22:30]]]).
- **Meaning-reconstruction** ([[grief-as-love-transformed]]) and
  **continuing bonds** practices ([[2026-05-14-session-reyes#^[30:08]]])
  are the empirically supported integrative-phase interventions
  appropriate once the avoidance cluster softens.
