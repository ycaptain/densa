## Narrative reconstruction

The session opens on a Mark whose pre-session week has been
*the same* — same 3:40 wakings, same chest band, same Wednesday
non-watching couch evening with Sarah. The new datum is the
Thursday-morning kitchen-table moment, which Mark himself
flags as *scaring him a little* because the watching-self
phenomenology is no longer confined to the workday — the
non-functional time is showing it too.

Reyes notices the between-session sentence Mark wrote: *"the
phone is heavier than the phone."* Rather than analyse it
cognitively, she asks Mark to say it out loud and notice what
happens in his body. The arm-heaviness response and brief
flutter in the chest band confirm the sentence is what she
calls a **body sentence** — the kind of artefact that bypasses
cognitive articulation and accesses the somatic register
directly. This is the somatic-anchoring entry point.

Reyes then pivots explicitly: *"I want to spend some time on
what's in your body, not just what's in your head."* The
intervention sequence that follows is recognisably IFS's 6Fs
applied in EFT-flavoured somatic language. Eyes-closed
attention to the chest band; the band becomes a *hand*
holding the chest down; the hand becomes a *foreman* — a
specific image with posture, age, and a job description in
Mark's own words. The protector's developmental anchor
crystallises at *~age 12*, the period of Mark's parents'
beginning to separate; the protector's job has been *keep Mark
running* for almost three decades.

Reyes explicitly names the IFS frame in the room — without
jargon — and offers the protector's framing back to Mark in
non-pathologising terms: *"he's not the enemy. He saved you.
He probably saved you at twelve. He's saving you right now."*
The clinical move is the opposite of trying to override the
protector; it is to **earn his trust** so that, eventually, the
material he's been guarding can be addressed in conditions of
safety.

The session's most loaded moment is the descent under the
protector's hand: Reyes asks *"underneath the foreman's hand
— what's there?"*. Mark first resists looking; with a small
push he glimpses the **cottage-cheese moment** from session 1
in fuller form — the unbearable ordinariness of his father's
last day. Mark's voice tightens. Reyes does not push; she asks
Mark to *let it sit* for a minute without changing it. The
contact lasts ~60 seconds. The chest band loosens in real
time, described as *"the hand knows we're looking and is
allowing it for a minute."*

Reyes closes the experiential work deliberately — *"that's
enough for today on this. We're not going to push past it"* —
and frames what just happened in clinical language so Mark can
carry it: a piece of grief the daily-running part has been
holding away from awareness was made briefly available in a
room safe enough to receive it. The foreman now has *a tiny
bit more evidence that he doesn't have to do the job alone.*
This is the operating principle of [[ifs]] in one sentence.

The remainder of the session covers:

- The Saturday 20-minute call held with mother — first partial
  shift of [[avoidant-mother-contact]].
- The Sarah-distance theme, signposted but not worked.
- Psychiatry consult logistics for 04-23 with Han, and Reyes's
  request that Mark relay the session's protector-frame work
  to Han so Han's medication-decision-making has the data.
- Mark's medication-reluctance disclosure (paternal-medication
  inheritance) and Reyes's response that there is a *sleep
  piece* question partially independent of the
  *depression-medication* question — pre-seeding the structure
  of the 04-23 conversation.

Reyes's between-session ask is, again, deliberately minimal:
**do not** try to recreate this work alone at the kitchen
table at 4:30 a.m. If the cottage-cheese material surfaces,
notice it, mark it ("I'll come back to you on Thursday"), let
the foreman do his job for the week.

## Working formulation

*(ifs lens)*

The session is a textbook 6-F sequence ([[ifs]]):
**Find** (somatic attention to the band) → **Focus** (allowed
attention without forcing change) → **Flesh out** (the hand
becomes the foreman, sleeves rolled, age-12 developmental
anchor, job description in Mark's words) → **Feel toward**
(Mark spontaneously articulates "he's not the enemy" — Self-
energy adequate) → **beFriend** (the protector's job is
described in his own terms, endorsed not flattened) →
**Fears** (the protector's fear is the chest "blowing open" if
the hand is removed, pointing at the Exile material
underneath, which becomes the cottage-cheese moment opened at
[18:01]). The session does **not** attempt unburdening; the
cottage-cheese material is *contacted*, not *worked through*.
That sequencing is deliberate and correct — unburdening on
first protector-contact is contraindicated, the protector
trust-base is too thin.

*(somatic lens)*

The somatic data carries the work. The chest band
([[somatic-grief-containment]]) is the on-ramp; the
band-as-hand-as-foreman sequence is a textbook somatic→
metaphor→meaning escalation. The session ends with the chest
band measurably looser in-room — Mark himself describing it
as *"the hand knows we're looking and is allowing it for a
minute."* The clinical principle is that the body has
information the cognitive system does not yet have access to;
work that *starts* in the body lands more reliably for clients
with masked-depression / containment presentations
([[masked-depression]]) than work that starts in cognition.

*(eft lens)*

The decisive EFT move is the **stay-with-it** instruction at
[18:01]: *"Stay with the cottage cheese for a few breaths.
Don't push it down, don't push it up. Just — let it sit."*
This is Greenberg's process-experiential operating principle —
affect is the unit of change, and the change comes from
**fully experiencing** the affect rather than analysing it. The
~60-second hold without pushing produces the somatic shift
(band looser at session close) and is consistent with the
EFT-grief literature's findings on grief-affect sequencing
(see also [[grief-as-love-transformed]]).

*(biopsychosocial-4P lens)*

Layer-by-layer delta from session 1:
**Predisposing**: substrate unchanged but more articulable now
— the foreman is the lived embodiment of the multi-generational
containment line. **Precipitating**: unchanged (still the
sudden bereavement + regret-loop). **Perpetuating**: small
positive shift — Saturday call held; weekly avoidance pattern
not yet broken but its absolute consistency is broken.
**Protective**: addition — the therapeutic relationship is
now part of the protective frame; the protector has
*permission* from outside the closed system to take occasional
breaks. This is itself a structural protective factor for
[[complicated-grief]] trajectory.
