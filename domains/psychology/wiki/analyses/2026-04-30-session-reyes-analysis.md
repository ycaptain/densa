## Three-layer formulation update (per session-1 frame)

The case formulation established in
[[2026-04-02-session-reyes-analysis]] (proximal grief / regret-loop /
containment style) does not change. What changes is the *protector*'s
position relative to the containment layer: it is no longer purely
implicit. The protector ("foreman") is now an addressable entity in
the room. The chair-work exercise does not weaken the protector — it
makes the protector's job visible to itself.

## Biopsychosocial 4P framing

- **Predisposing**: Manager-part anchored to age ~12 (parental
  separation context, mother on the kitchen floor for two days,
  client became the household functional point). This is the
  developmental moment the protector references in its own voice
  during the chair-work. The protector's skepticism toward "by who?"
  is *not* about the present-day system (Sarah, Reyes, Han); it
  inherits from the summer the system manifestly *did not* hold for
  Mark's mother.
- **Precipitating**: father's death (proximal cue intact from
  session 1).
- **Perpetuating**: the protector's "by who?" challenge. The
  protector is not refusing to step aside; it is refusing to step
  aside *without evidence that someone competent will cover the
  shift*. The pattern named in [[avoidant-mother-contact]] is one
  surface of this question.
- **Protective**: (a) the spousal system held on Sunday — Sarah
  receiving the father's-blood-pressure-medications disclosure
  without trying to fix; (b) the cross-clinical model coherence
  (Reyes and Han running compatible IFS frames); (c) Mark's own
  capacity to articulate the chair-work's emotional truth
  retrospectively at [14:45] ("I knew his answers before I said
  them").

## What Reyes does (technique-level)

1. **Begins with body**, not technique ([04:20]). Locates the
   chest-band where it sat at the end of session 2 and confirms its
   modulation in real time before introducing anything new. This is
   somatic continuity — Reyes does not jump frames at the top of
   the session.
2. **Names the exercise out loud, asks consent, leaves an exit ramp**
   ([07:00]–[09:00]). The chair-work is a willingness-bounded move,
   not an ambush. The "we can stop at any time" framing is operating
   per IFS protocol's no-coercion principle.
3. **Two-chair unblending proper** ([09:15]–[20:00]). Mark sits in
   the original chair, the foreman in the empty chair. Mark asks a
   question, switches chairs, answers as the foreman, switches back.
   Reyes intervenes only to (a) keep the back-and-forth moving and
   (b) hold the protector's developmental anchor visible when it
   surfaces.
4. **Catches the "by who?" challenge as the central question, not
   as resistance** ([14:45]). The protector's skepticism here is the
   honest face of the avoidant-mother-contact system. Reyes does not
   try to answer the protector's question in the moment; she reflects
   it back ("stay with that question") and leaves it open.
5. **Reframes the prior week's events as evidence the protector is
   collecting** ([18:30]–[19:30]). The Saturday call with Mark's
   mother (held, decompressed on the back porch with Sarah's beer)
   is not described as "progress" but as *data the foreman has*. The
   re-framing is precise: the protector is not being asked to
   believe Sarah and Reyes; the protector is being asked to look at
   accumulating evidence on its own terms.
6. **Direct address with somatic checkpoint** ([20:00]). Reyes asks
   Mark to address the protector in his own voice. Mark's address —
   *"I see what you've been doing. I'm not firing you."* — is
   followed within 5 seconds by an observable somatic shift (chest
   band loosens; the "hand" rests rather than grips). Reyes flags
   this as a within-session repair event but explicitly cautions
   it will not durably hold.
7. **Closes with low-stakes between-session reps**, not high-stakes
   homework ([31:00]). The 5-second check-in (*foreman, what are
   you doing right now?*) is calibrated so that the protector's
   unblending muscle can train without anyone — protector included —
   having to renegotiate the system's stability.

## Critique / what could have gone differently

- The "by who?" challenge is the central question the session
  surfaces but Reyes deliberately leaves it open. A more directive
  clinician might have offered "Reyes, Han, Sarah" as candidate
  answers in the moment. Reyes's choice is consistent with IFS
  protocol (the answer must come from the client's own evidence,
  not the clinician's reassurance), and the subsequent two-week
  interval bears this out — the session-4 release happens because
  the protector accumulates its *own* evidence, not because the
  protector was told to trust the system.
- The Theo's-drawing material at [27:00] is correctly held rather
  than worked; a less experienced clinician might have rushed it.
- The "Mom didn't have anyone either" insight at [29:00] could
  have been amplified. Reyes flags it for future work — appropriate
  given the session's existing affective load — but the insight is
  important enough that it deserves a re-entry early in session 4.
