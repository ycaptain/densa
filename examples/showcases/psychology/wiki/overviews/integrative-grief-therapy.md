# Integrative grief therapy (the framework operating across this arc)

> [!important] Overview one-liner (v1 framework)
> The integrative approach Dr. Reyes operates in this arc:
> braids **Emotion-Focused Therapy** (Greenberg / Johnson — affect
> as the unit of change), **Internal Family Systems** (Schwartz —
> protectors and exiles), **attachment theory** (Bowlby / Ainsworth
> — relational template as substrate), and
> **meaning-reconstruction** (Neimeyer / continuing-bonds literature
> — narrative integration), with **somatic** entry points used when
> verbal access is constrained. Not a single proprietary modality;
> the dominant pattern in contemporary grief work outside
> manualised CGT (Shear et al.).

This page describes the framework as it *appears in this arc*, with
explicit pointers to which sub-modality is doing the load-bearing
work in which session.

## Why "integrative" and not a single modality

No single grief framework handles all of the following at once:

- **Affective access** when the client's somatic / containment
  baseline limits verbal description (EFT + somatic).
- **Intra-psychic structure** when a strong protector part
  organises against affect contact (IFS).
- **Relational substrate** when the loss is shaped by the
  attachment history with the deceased (attachment theory).
- **Narrative integration** once the affect has surfaced enough
  to be metabolised (meaning-reconstruction + continuing-bonds).

Manualised CGT (Shear's Complicated Grief Treatment, the
gold-standard 16-session protocol for PGD) does combine several
of these, but is designed for cases that meet the 12-month PGD
duration criterion. For early (months 2-6) intervention on PGD
trajectories, an integrative non-manualised approach is the
standard-of-care reach.

## The four sub-modalities — what each contributes here

### Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT)

- **Provenance**: Leslie Greenberg (process-experiential), Sue
  Johnson (EFT for couples).
- **Core claim**: affect is the unit of change; cognition follows.
  Sessions are organised around making affect available, in the
  room, in real time.
- **In this arc**: the somatic-anchoring work in session 2 is
  classic EFT — letting the client *feel* the chest band rather
  than *describe* it ([[2026-04-16-session-reyes]] [04:38]).
  The "stay with the cottage cheese for a few breaths"
  intervention ([[2026-04-16-session-reyes]] [18:01]) is the EFT
  *sequential* move: contact → allow → metabolise.

### Internal Family Systems (IFS)

See [[ifs]] for the full framework. In this arc IFS contributes:

- The Manager-Exile-Firefighter terminology that lets Reyes and
  Mark *name* the foreman as a part with a job, rather than
  treating the containment behaviour as resistance.
- The clinical sequence (6 Fs) used implicitly in session 2.
- The unburdening logic that fits the session-4 partial
  resolution of the guilt-charge.

### Attachment theory

- **Provenance**: John Bowlby, Mary Ainsworth; contemporary
  reach: Mikulincer & Shaver's adult-attachment research.
- **Core claim**: early caregiving experiences encode a working
  model of relational availability that organises adult emotional
  regulation, relationship behaviour, and grief response.
- **In this arc**: the three-layer formulation Reyes offers in
  session 1 ([[2026-04-02-session-reyes]] [26:40]) is essentially
  an attachment-theoretic reading. The "long-running pattern of
  emotional containment" is named as inherited — generations of
  men in the lineage with unspoken affect. Mark's
  *avoidant-protective* attachment strategy (in adult-attachment
  terms, *dismissing*) maps onto his current behaviour with his
  mother, with Sarah, and historically with his father.

### Meaning-reconstruction + continuing bonds

See [[grief-as-love-transformed]] for the concept. In this arc:

- Introduced **only** at session 4, not earlier — deliberately.
  Meaning-reconstruction work *imposed* before the affect
  contact has happened is hollow.
- Continuing-bond practices (walk in meaningful places, letter
  unsent, talking out loud) offered at session 4 as optional
  between-session experiments
  ([[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [30:08]).

## Operating principles visible in this arc

The framework's *operating principles* — the cross-cutting
clinical stances that show up across every session — are more
informative than the sub-modality list.

### 1. Pacing dictated by the protector

The Manager-part determines the floor of how fast affect contact
can proceed. Sessions 1 and 4 both honour this: session 1 does
not attempt affect contact at all (the protector is too active);
session 4 does meaning-reconstruction *after* the protector has
allowed unburdening. Pushing past the Manager is contraindicated;
it produces compliance without integration.

### 2. Process work owns process; psychiatry owns assessment + somatic floor

Cross-clinical boundary, explicit in this arc:

- Dr. Reyes (therapy): process material, protector dynamics,
  affect contact, meaning work.
- Dr. Han (psychiatry): symptom severity scales (PHQ-9, PCL-5,
  GAD-7, DES-II), differential diagnosis (MDD vs. PGD vs. PTSD),
  medication decision-making, sleep treatment.

The two clinicians do not duplicate each other's work. Reyes
does not try to medicate; Han does not do process work in his
chair. They *coordinate* via brief written notes
([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han]] [00:04],
[[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [16:42] — Reyes asks Mark to bring
the session content back to Han).

### 3. The somatic body as the channel of first resort

Mark's verbal vocabulary for affect is limited (masked
depression presentation, [[masked-depression]]). The framework
uses the body as the primary input/output channel:

- Session 1: notice the chest tightness; do not analyse it
  cognitively ([[2026-04-02-session-reyes]] [12:08]).
- Session 2: chest tightness becomes the "hand" becomes the
  "foreman" — somatic → parts metaphor → meaning
  ([[2026-04-16-session-reyes]] [04:38]).
- Session 4: the chest is now "the foreman sitting on a bucket"
  — somatic shift readable in real time
  ([[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [06:30]).

This is a deliberate sequencing: somatic *precedes* cognitive
articulation because the cognitive vocabulary is partly what is
missing.

### 4. Avoidance cluster as the per-week marker

The clinically tracked outcome variable across sessions is
**avoidance-cluster behaviour** — specifically the
[[avoidant-mother-contact]] pattern. Loosening of this pattern
is the most reliable indicator of trajectory change, more so
than PHQ-9 scores or self-reported mood, because avoidance is
behaviourally observable and changes before subjective mood does.

### 5. The protector is an ally to be redeployed, not removed

Echoes the IFS stance. Across the four sessions, the foreman is
*never* asked to stand down. The clinical move is to give him
**different information** so he can do his job differently.
This is the principle underlying the entire arc's directionality
([[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [12:14]).

## When this framework is the wrong reach

- **Pre-stabilisation cases.** Active suicidality, heavy
  Firefighter activity (substance escalation, self-harm),
  thought-disorder presentations require stabilisation work first;
  the integrative grief framework operates above a stability
  floor.
- **Cases without an identifiable protector to address.** Some
  grief presentations are flat-affect *because of* primary
  depressive process, not because of containment dynamics. SSRI
  trial may need to precede process work.
- **Cases beyond the 12-month PGD threshold with consolidated
  syndrome.** Manualised CGT (Shear) is the gold-standard reach,
  with stronger RCT evidence than the integrative approach.
