## The question, refined

What is the right framing for the trade-off between **early
pharmacology** and **active process work** in a case of
sub-12-month bereavement-related depressive presentation with
[[complicated-grief]] risk markers? Three nested sub-questions:

1. **In a specific case** (Mark's), did the conservative-hold
   decision at 04-23 turn out to be the right call? *Will be
   evaluable at mid-July ~12-week post-loss.*
2. **Across the worked-example arc**, can the wiki's
   coordination machinery make this trade-off *audit-able* —
   i.e. produce a record of *why* the decision was made when
   it was made, against which markers, with what
   counter-considerations? *Yes, demonstrated by
   [[medication-arc]] timeline + reasoning columns.*
3. **As a general clinical question** beyond this case, what
   are the conditions under which deferring SSRI initiation
   in early bereavement is the right call, versus the
   conditions under which earlier initiation produces better
   outcomes? *Open generally; this case is one data point in
   a long-running clinical literature.*

The wiki tracks (1) and (2) as the case develops; (3) is a
question the case *informs* without being able to *answer*
single-case.

## Why this question matters

Conservative prescribing in early bereavement is a clinical
stance with genuine pros and cons. Counter-considerations to
the *grief is informational* stance Han endorsed at 04-23:

- **SSRI-induced symptom reduction** can make process work
  *more* possible by lowering the affective load enough that
  the client can sustain therapy session by session.
- **Sleep deprivation at 5h / night for 8+ weeks** is a known
  contributor to depressive symptom load; failing to treat the
  sleep can mean the apparent depressive picture is
  partly sleep-deprivation-pretending-to-be-grief, and
  treating it pharmacologically can clarify the differential.
- **Risk of PGD consolidation** at 6-12 months is non-trivial
  in cases with all four risk markers present (this case has
  them all); waiting until 12 weeks to revisit the SSRI
  question presumes the active process work will produce the
  shift that earlier pharmacology might have produced more
  reliably.

Han's case-specific reasoning ([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han]] [22:30])
turns on (a) active engagement with process work (Reyes's IFS
work is *landing*, per Han's read of the foreman framing),
(b) absence of closed-loop features (no SI, no work
decompensation, intact partner-system), and (c) client's
informed med-reluctance based on a reasonable biographical
substrate (paternal medication history). In *another* case
where any of those conditions changed, the same psychiatrist
would credibly initiate.

The question is **not** "is conservative or active prescribing
better in general." The question is "what's the auditable
chain of reasoning that connects this case's specific features
to this case's specific decision, and what markers would
trigger the decision to be revisited?"

## What would close the question

For sub-question (1) — the case-specific call:

- **The conservative-hold was right** if, at mid-July review,
  avoidance cluster has materially loosened (per
  [[can-grief-be-spoken-with-mother]] markers), sleep has
  improved enough to not require trazodone, and the
  depressive picture has *not* consolidated.
- **The conservative-hold was wrong** if any of: (a) avoidance
  cluster is unchanged or worsened; (b) the system has
  decompensated (work, partner, parental); (c) active SI has
  emerged; (d) sleep has deteriorated under 4h/night
  sustained. In any of these, the SSRI question becomes
  *active* and the deferred-three-months window will look in
  retrospect like time lost.

The "right" answer is intentionally **bi-conditional** rather
than uni-directional. Both outcomes are real possibilities.
The worked example does not foreknow which will obtain; the
3-6 month follow-on is the falsifying period.

For sub-question (2) — the architectural call: the question
is *already closed positively*. The wiki's coordination
machinery has produced an audit-able record of the
2026-04-23 decision with full rationale, pre-named markers,
pre-named decision dates, and the cross-clinical channel
that fed it. See [[medication-arc]] timeline.

For sub-question (3) — the general clinical question: the
question stays `open` perpetually. The clinical literature
has decades of accumulating data on bereavement
pharmacology; one case is not adjudicating it. The arc
*informs* by demonstrating one chain of reasoning at scale,
not by *settling* the general question.

## What would change the question

- **A change in the client's safety picture** (active SI, panic-
  level somatic episodes, sleep <3h sustained) immediately
  re-opens the medication question on a different time
  scale.
- **A change in process-work engagement** (sustained
  session-cancellation, regression to baseline avoidance,
  Manager-part refusing the framing) would change the
  conditions Han's conservative reasoning rested on.
- **A change in the diagnostic picture** (e.g. emergence of
  ASD / ADHD features previously absent, or formal PGD
  diagnosis at the 12-month mark) would reorganise the
  question's framing entirely.
