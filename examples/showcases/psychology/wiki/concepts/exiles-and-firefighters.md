# IFS parts terminology — Managers, Exiles, Firefighters

> [!important] What this concept names
> Three of the role-categories Internal Family Systems (Richard
> Schwartz, 1995) uses to describe sub-personalities (*parts*).
> Every person has a multiplicity of parts; IFS does not pathologise
> this — what's pathological is **part-extreme-roles** in response
> to early burdens, not the multiplicity itself.

The three roles, briefly:

- **Managers** — proactive *protectors*. Run the day-to-day. Plan,
  control, perfect, achieve, caretake. Try to *prevent* exile
  activation. Strongly socially-acceptable; often invisible to
  the person hosting them.
- **Exiles** — the *wounded* / vulnerable parts. Hold the original
  pain (shame, fear, grief, abandonment). Usually young.
  Managers and Firefighters work to keep Exiles out of awareness.
- **Firefighters** — reactive *protectors*. Activate *after* an
  Exile breaches awareness. Their job is to suppress the
  overwhelm fast, by any means available — substances, dissociation,
  rage, distraction, bingeing.

The clinical move in IFS is not to fight protectors but to **earn
their permission** to access the Exiles they're guarding, so the
Exiles can be unburdened and the protectors can be re-deployed to
non-extreme roles.

### Firefighter activity (minimal but present)

Firefighters are not the dominant pattern here, but two
low-intensity behaviours are functionally Firefighter-shaped:

- **Two beers most evenings** since the bereavement — up from
  baseline of weekends only ([[2026-04-23-psychiatry-han]] [02:18]).
  Modest enough that Han endorses watching for creep rather than
  intervening.
- **The "watching dumb TV" couch evenings** functioning as
  attentional distraction rather than connection
  ([[2026-04-02-session-reyes]] [03:47]). Reyes's session-4
  laundry-room moment ([[2026-05-14-session-reyes]] [12:14])
  is partly the breakage of this Firefighter pattern.

A useful clinical observation: the *absence* of strong Firefighter
activity (no substance escalation, no rage, no dissociation
beyond mild attentional flattening) is part of why Reyes can
hold a slower-paced experiential approach. In a presentation with
heavier Firefighter activity (e.g. binge drinking, self-harm,
explosive interpersonal episodes), stabilisation work would
precede the cottage-cheese-style Exile-contact intervention.

## Why the terminology matters in this arc

IFS gives Reyes and Mark a **shared vocabulary** that does not
pathologise the protector. Compare:

- *Without IFS framing*: "your coping mechanism is avoidance and
  you need to feel your feelings." This frames the Manager as
  *the problem* and produces resistance — the part doing 70% of
  the daily functional load is told to stand down.
- *With IFS framing*: "this part of you has been doing a very hard
  job alone for a long time, and we're going to let him take
  breaks." The Manager is recognised as an ally that has been
  over-deployed, not as a defect to be removed.

The latter framing is what made the 04-16 session land. The
client noted unprompted that the foreman "saved" him and that the
work was about earning the foreman's permission, not overriding
him ([[2026-04-16-session-reyes]] [14:55]).
